# 3.2 Command Flow

## Scope
- Focuses on instruction fulfillment flows.
- Includes processes for issuing, tracking, and completing commands during the session.

### 3.2.1 Client Instructions
#### 3.2.1.1 "Come Here" Instruction
- **Purpose:**
    - This feature allows the client to summon the companions to come closer without shouting or calling them.
    - It is useful when the companions are away (within 10-15 meters) but still in sight.

- **Functionality:**
    - The client will see a "Come Here" button on the in-service screen.
    - When pressed, a notification is sent to both companions.
    - The Captain Companion must accept the instruction first, followed by the Vice Companion.
    - If the Captain does not respond within a configurable time limit (default: 7 seconds), the system will escalate to Notification Level 2, which is an automated phone call to the Captain. If the Captain still does not respond, the system will escalate to the admin.
    - Once accepted, both companions will approach the client.
    - The client will see a confirmation that the companions have accepted the instruction.

- **Companion Response:**
    - If the companions are already near the client, they can respond to the instruction as "Already Here."
    - In such cases, the companions will verbally inform the client that they are already present.

- **Logging:**
    - The system will log all "Come Here" instructions, including the instruction object, responses, and outcomes, for future analysis or troubleshooting.

- **Use Case:**
    - This feature is an alternative to shouting or calling the companions when the client wants them to come closer.

- **Edge Cases:**
    - If the companions are unable to approach the client (e.g., physical barriers), this will be moved to open questions.
