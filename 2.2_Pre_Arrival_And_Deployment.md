# 2.2 Client Matching & Deployment

## Scope
- The end-to-end flow of the client meeting the activated Companion Duo and starting the service session.
- Covers: client notification, location sharing, meeting point setup, companion movement, QR handshake, and deployment start.
- This is the **client-facing** counterpart to 2.1 (Companion Self-Matching). It begins once the Duo is activated and ends when the QR handshake starts the session clock.
- Prerequisite: Duo must be in `ACTIVATED` state (completed in 2.1).

### 2.2 Client Matching & Deployment

#### 2.2.1 Client Matching
* **Overview:** Client matching ensures the companions are ready to meet the client and begin deployment.

##### 2.2.1.1 Client Notification
* When the Duo is active and the booking time is less than 30 minutes, the client will receive a notification: "Your companions are ready and waiting for your instructions."
* The notification includes the following options for the client:
    * **Share Location:** Allows the companions to track the client’s real-time location. This helps companions find the client faster.
    * **Set Meeting Point:** Enables the client to specify a meeting point by either:
        * Taking a picture of a nearby store (using the **Upload Picture** button).
        * Providing manual instructions (using the **Update Info** button).
* The client can update their response directly in the application.
* **Escalation:**
    * If the client does not respond within 10 minutes, a follow-up notification is sent.
    * After 20 minutes of no response, the system triggers an automated call to the client.
* **15-Minute Window (T+15m Auto-Complete):**
    * If the client does not show up and complete the QR handshake with the companions within **15 minutes after the scheduled booking time**, the booking is **automatically marked as completed**.
    * No refund is issued. Companions are released and free for their next assignment.
    * The client is freed to make a new booking.
    * During this 15-minute window, companions are instructed to stay put and wait at the meeting point.

##### 2.2.1.2 Client Companion meetup

###### 2.2.1.2.1 Location Sharing
* Once the client enters the venue premises, their location will start showing to the Captain Companion.
* If the client declines location access:
    * The client will be prompted to update their location manually in an input box.
    * An option will be provided to upload an image of the nearest store (e.g., using the **Upload Picture** button).
    * A message box at the top will display: "Sharing your location helps companions locate you faster."
    * Companions will rely on the text input and any images provided by the client to locate them.

###### 2.2.1.2.2 Companion View
* Companions will see a screen similar to a ride-hailing app, showing their current location and the client’s location (if shared) on the map.
* This information will be displayed on the booking screen, with real-time updates on their position.

###### 2.2.1.2.3 Admin Contact and Notifications
* Companions will have an option to notify the client by pressing a button in the app.
* If the client remains unresponsive, contacting the admin will be the last resort.

###### 2.2.1.2.4 Time Constraints
* After the companion duo is activated, there will be a 30-minute window for client matching to be completed.
* If the client does not respond within this time, an escalation ladder will be triggered for the next 15 minutes.
* If no resolution is reached after 45 minutes, the booking will progress, and it will be the client’s responsibility to respond.

##### 2.2.1.3 Client Meeting Point
* The client is required to set a meeting point. This can be done by:
    * Taking a picture of a nearby store.
    * Mentioning their location manually if location access is not granted.

##### 2.2.1.4 Companion Movement
* The companions will start making their way to the client based on the location information provided.

##### 2.2.1.5 Deployment Start
* Once the companions meet the client, the Captain Companion will scan the QR code provided by the client.
* Scanning the QR code officially begins the deployment.
